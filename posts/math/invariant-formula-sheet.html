Invariant Formula Sheet

A reference page collecting formulas and theorems that seem central to the invariant subsection: fixed points, contraction, Lyapunov ideas, forward-invariant sets, constrained optimization, and the geometric intuition behind symplectic dynamics.

Read the main essay Back to Math archive

Why this page exists

The main essay is conceptual. This page is meant to be the mathematical companion: a compact reference for statements, formulas, and structural ideas that keep returning whenever one thinks about invariance, stability, and constrained evolution.

Fixed points and contraction

Contraction mapping

A map contracts distances and therefore forces iterates together.

\[ \exists c \in (0,1) \quad \text{such that} \quad d(Tx,Ty)\le c\, d(x,y) \]

Banach fixed-point theorem

On a complete metric space, a contraction has a unique fixed point.

\[ \exists!\,x^\ast \in X \quad \text{such that} \quad T(x^\ast)=x^\ast \]

Iterative convergence

Repeated application of the map converges to the fixed point.

\[ x_{n+1}=T(x_n), \qquad x_n \to x^\ast \]

Error decay under contraction

The distance to the fixed point shrinks geometrically.

\[ d(x_n,x^\ast)\le c^n d(x_0,x^\ast) \]

Invariant sets and stability

Forward-invariant set

Once a trajectory enters the set, it remains there.

\[ x_0\in S \implies x_t\in S \quad \forall t\ge 0 \]

Invariant manifold intuition

Dynamics preserve a lower-dimensional geometric structure.

\[ \Phi_t(M)\subseteq M \]

Lyapunov decrease

A Lyapunov function tracks one-sided motion toward stability.

\[ V(x_{t+1}) \le V(x_t) \] \[ \dot V(x) \le 0 \]

Lipschitz control

Local perturbations remain bounded under the map.

\[ \|f(x)-f(x')\|\le K\|x-x'\| \]

Optimization and constrained learning

Empirical risk minimization

The baseline objective that the invariant viewpoint is pushing against.

\[ \min_{\theta}\; \mathbb{E}_{(x,y)\sim \mathcal D}\big[L(f_\theta(x),y)\big] \]

Safe-region reinterpretation

Replace asymptotic perfection with bounded admissible behavior.

\[ \forall t,\quad e(t)\in E_{\text{safe}} \]

SGD update

Standard stochastic descent as a discrete-time dynamical system.

\[ \theta_{t+1}=\theta_t-\eta \,\nabla \ell(\theta_t;\xi_t) \]

Projected SGD

A direct way of preserving feasibility after each update.

\[ \tilde\theta_{t+1}=\theta_t-\eta \,\nabla \ell(\theta_t;\xi_t) \] \[ \theta_{t+1}=\Pi_C(\tilde\theta_{t+1}) \]

Penalty and Lagrangian methods

Violations are discouraged or dualized during optimization.

\[ L(\theta)+\lambda \,\max(0,g(\theta)) \] \[ \mathcal L(\theta,\alpha)=L(\theta)+\alpha\, g(\theta) \]

Barrier-style viewpoint

A forbidden region can be encoded through a function that blows up near the boundary.

\[ \min_{\theta}\; L(\theta)+\mu\, B(\theta), \qquad B(\theta)\to \infty \text{ near } \partial C \]

Stochastic differential view of SGD

A continuous-time approximation that emphasizes noisy flow rather than exact convergence.

\[ d\theta_t = -\nabla F(\theta_t)\,dt + \Sigma(\theta_t)\, dW_t \]

Linear regression toy model

A minimal example in which constrained dynamics can already be studied.

\[ \hat y = w^\top x \] \[ \ell(w;(x,y)) = \tfrac12 (w^\top x-y)^2 \] \[ w_{t+1}=w_t-\eta (w_t^\top x_t-y_t)x_t \]

Hamiltonian and symplectic structure

Classical symplectic geometry is probably not the first formal language for your learning question, but it still matters here because it is the canonical example of dynamics governed by exact preserved structure.

Hamilton's equations

Evolution is determined by a Hamiltonian \(H(q,p)\).

\[ \dot q_i = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_i}, \qquad \dot p_i = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q_i} \]

Symplectic form

The canonical geometric object preserved by Hamiltonian flow.

\[ \omega = \sum_{i=1}^n dq_i \wedge dp_i \]

Symplectic invariance

The flow preserves the symplectic form.

\[ \Phi_t^\ast \omega = \omega \]

Energy conservation

For autonomous Hamiltonian systems, the Hamiltonian is constant along trajectories.

\[ \frac{d}{dt}H(q(t),p(t)) = 0 \]

Poisson bracket

A quantity is conserved if its Poisson bracket with the Hamiltonian vanishes.

\[ \{F,H\}=0 \implies \frac{d}{dt}F=0 \]

Symplectic integrator intuition

Numerical methods can preserve geometric structure even when they do not preserve exact trajectories.

\[ \Psi_h^\ast \omega = \omega \]

What seems most relevant for future work

Most directly usable

Contraction mappings, fixed points, forward-invariant sets, Lyapunov functions, projected SGD, penalties, and barrier methods.

Conceptually motivating

Hamiltonian flow, symplectic preservation, and geometric integrators. These sharpen the idea of structure-preserving evolution even if they are not the first tools to deploy in learning theory.
